Physics handbook
The physics of densification
What every buyer should understand before talking to a vendor. Read this once — you will never be fooled by a TPH claim again.
01 — Fundamental physics
⟳
What the machine actually does
Forces loose biomass through die holes under extreme sustained pressure. Lignin — the natural polymer binder in plant cells — softens at 80–130°C from friction heat, flows into voids, and re-solidifies on cooling. No lignin activation = no durable pellet. Everything else is secondary.
⊗
Torque is the real metric, not kW
Power = Torque × RPM ÷ 9,550. A pellet mill needs HIGH torque at LOW die speed (100–200 RPM). Two machines with identical kW but different gearbox ratios exert completely different force at the die face. Always ask for gearbox output torque in Nm.
≋
Moisture: the hidden control variable
10–14% moisture at mill inlet = optimal. Below 8%: die overheats, pellets crack and dust. Above 16%: steam flash in holes, no bonding, pellets crumble on exit. Every 1% off the optimal costs 5–8% throughput. All manufacturer TPH claims assume exactly 12%.
∥
L/D ratio — compression ratio
Die hole depth ÷ hole diameter. Higher ratio = more compression = stronger pellet = more kWh consumed per tonne. Straw needs 5:1–7:1. Bamboo and hardwood need 7:1–10:1. Using wood-spec L/D on straw = blocked die and motor trips. Using straw-spec L/D on wood = crumbly pellets that fail transit.
Actual TPH = (Motor kW × Transmission efficiency) ÷ SEC (kWh/tonne)
— Transmission efficiency: direct drive 94%, poly-V belt 90%, V-belt 88%
— SEC (Specific Energy Consumption): rice/wheat straw 38–60 kWh/t · cotton stalk 65–90 · bamboo 80–130 · softwood sawdust 35–50 · hardwood 65–95
02 — Ring die vs flat die
Ring die — industrial standard

Die is a hollow cylinder (drum) that rotates. Rollers are fixed inside. Material is fed radially through the die wall. Pressure field is uniform across the full die width. Suitable for fibrous biomass at any scale. Maximum practical die size ~1,000mm. One machine can produce 1–15 t/h. All serious European manufacturers (Andritz, CPM, Salmatec, Bühler) use ring die exclusively. Gearbox output torque: 8,000–80,000 Nm depending on size.

Flat die — small scale and budget

Die is a round flat plate. Rollers press down from above. Pressure field is non-uniform — centre vs edge of plate differs by up to 30%. Throughput ceiling ~1.5 t/h per machine due to die area limits. Works adequately on sawdust. Struggles significantly with long-fibre agri-residue (straw, cotton stalk). Most budget Chinese and Indian-branded machines are flat die. If you need >3 t/h, you need multiple flat-die units — which costs more than one ring die.

The flat-die-as-ring-die misrepresentation — how to catch it

Some vendors show ring die machine photos in brochures but quote and ship flat die machines. The distinction is physical: a ring die's die assembly is a vertical hollow cylinder you can look through. A flat die's die is a horizontal plate lying flat — like a grinding wheel. Ask for an unedited video of the machine running, showing the die orientation clearly. If the die rotates horizontally (like a spinning plate), it is a flat die regardless of what the brochure or invoice states. Demand this video before placing any deposit.

The "10 TPH plant" claim — what it actually means

A Chinese vendor quoting 10 TPH from a single 260 kW ring die pellet mill is testing on pine sawdust at 12% moisture. Your Indian mixed crop residue from the same machine will give 3.5–5.5 t/h. For a genuine 10 TPH plant on Indian agri-residue, you need 2× 260 kW mills (ring die, in parallel) or 1× 520 kW mill with very consistent, pre-characterised feedstock.

05 — Questions that vendors cannot fake
Technical demands — ask these first

1. On which exact material, moisture %, and particle size was the claimed TPH tested?
2. What is the gearbox output shaft torque in Nm at rated motor RPM?
3. What is the die RPM at full rated load?
4. What L/D ratio die are you supplying for my specific feedstock?
5. What PDI (Pellet Durability Index) was achieved at the claimed TPH?
6. Show me the motor amp draw log at full load during commissioning — not the nameplate rating.
7. For ring die claim: show cross-section engineering drawing of die assembly.

Commercial demands — put in contract

1. Performance guarantee clause: minimum TPH on YOUR specific feedstock at YOUR moisture, with penalty for shortfall.
2. Die + roller warranty: minimum 2,000 operating hours or 12 months, whichever later.
3. Commissioning support: named engineer on-site, minimum 7 days, at vendor cost.
4. Spare parts: rollers, die shell, bearings in India within 72 hours.
5. Reference visit: operating plant in India on same feedstock before deposit.
