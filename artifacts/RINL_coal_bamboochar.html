PCI coal parameters — what RINL tests, what acceptable levels are, why each matters

PCI (Pulverized Coal Injection) coal is injected directly into the blast furnace tuyeres as a partial substitute for coke. Unlike coking coal, it does not need to form coke — it needs to combust efficiently, grind easily, and not introduce harmful impurities into the hot metal. This makes PCI specs in some ways more accessible for alternative carbon sources like bamboo char.

Summary of PCI coal quality hierarchy: Sulphur and Phosphorus are absolute hard limits (safety/quality of steel). Ash and GCV drive economics. HGI determines operational throughput. AFT, Alkalies, and Chlorine determine long-term furnace health. VM/FC ratio determines coke substitution efficiency.

Coking coal parameters — full breakdown

Coking coal is fundamentally different from PCI coal — it must form metallurgical coke in the coke ovens, which means it needs specific plastic/rheological properties when heated to 1000°C+. This is why coking coal specs include parameters like CSN, Gieseler Plastometer, vitrinite reflectance, and Total Dilation — none of which are relevant to PCI. Bamboo char is completely irrelevant to the coking coal category.

Key insight: Most coking coal parameters (CSN, Gieseler, Gray King, Vitrinite reflectance, Dilation) measure the coal's plastic/coking behaviour — properties that bamboo char can never have because it has already been carbonized. Bamboo char is entirely irrelevant to the coking coal category. Any bamboo-in-steel-plant strategy must focus exclusively on PCI substitution or other reducing agent applications, never on coke oven blending.

The two carbon doors — the single idea that explains everything

A blast furnace has two entirely separate carbon inputs, and they never connect. Every question about where bamboo char fits collapses into one question: which door?

Door 1 — Top charge (closed to bamboo)
Lumps only
Ore, sinter, limestone and coke are dumped in from the top and sink down over 6–8 hours. Everything here must be big, hard, and load-bearing — it holds up 30 metres of burden. Coking coal feeds this door via the coke ovens.
Door 2 — Tuyere injection (open to bamboo)
Powder only
20–40 nozzles (tuyeres) around the base blast hot air at 1200°C. Powdered fuel is blown in with it, ignites in the raceway fireball, and is fully consumed in 20–50 milliseconds. PCI coal, petcoke, and bamboo char all compete for this slot.

Door 1 — Coking coal → coke (structural carbon)

Coking coal is not burned as coal. It is baked in a coke oven at ~1100°C for ~18 hours with no air. It melts, fuses, swells and re-solidifies into hard porous lumps of metallurgical coke (40–80mm).

Coke does 3 jobsCan an injectant replace it?
1. Fuel — burns to generate heatYes — PCI/petcoke/biochar all do this
2. Reductant — supplies carbon to strip oxygen from iron oreYes — injectants do this too
3. Load-bearing skeleton — physically holds the burden apart so gas rises and molten iron drainsNO — nothing else in the furnace does this job. This is why you can never go to 100% injection with any material, ever.
Why Door 1 is permanently closed to bamboo char: Coke gets its strength from passing through a plastic (molten) phase at 350–550°C, where separate particles fuse into one continuous mass. Bamboo char has already been pyrolyzed in your kiln — it is thermally dead. Reheat it and nothing happens: no softening, no swelling, no fusion. It scores zero on CSN, zero dilation, zero Gieseler fluidity. And even if it could fuse, it's a powder — it can never become a load-bearing lump. Stop thinking about coking coal.

Door 2 — The injectants (fuel + reductant only)

Through the tuyeres, plants inject powdered carbon to replace some of the coke. Every kg injected saves roughly 0.85–0.95 kg of coke. Since coke costs far more than coal, this is pure margin — which is why every modern blast furnace does it.

Strategic verdict — what this means for Bombay Bioenergy

This RINL tender (GEM/2025/B/6850356) has zero commercial relevance to Bombay Bioenergy. It is a lab sampling services contract and cannot be bid on by a pellet/biochar manufacturer. Do not pursue it.
The underlying PCI coal market is highly relevant and warrants serious strategic attention. RINL imports 12.88 lakh MT/year of PCI coal alone — and India's four major integrated steel plants (RINL/VSP, SAIL Bhilai/Bokaro/Durgapur/Rourkela, Tata Steel Jamshedpur/Kalinganagar, JSW Vijayanagar) collectively import and consume 15–20 million MT of PCI coal annually. The decarbonisation imperative and Green Steel pressure from EU buyers creates a genuine pull for bio-PCI. The actual entry point is approaching steel plant procurement teams with bio-PCI char product, not the testing tender.

What bamboo char genuinely offers for PCI

DimensionBamboo char position
Ash contentMajor advantage — 1.8–4.6% vs coal's 8–12%. Lower ash = less slag volume, productivity improvement
SulfurMajor advantage — 0.05–0.15% vs coal's 0.5–1.5%. Less desulfurisation cost downstream
Carbon neutralityRenewable/bio-based — meets EU Green Deal / CBAM requirements for green steel exports. This premium is emerging and real
GCVAdequate at 700–800°C carbonization (~26–30 MJ/kg). Lower than premium anthracite but comparable to HV PCI coal. Must compensate for lower energy density vs coal with pricing
Alkali content (K)Significant risk. Must be quantified for B. bambos and D. strictus before any commercial claim is made. If K₂O on ash exceeds 3%, blending rate must be capped at levels where total BF alkali input remains within tolerance (~5–10% blend share at most)

Non-negotiable next steps before approaching any steel plant

#ActionWhy critical
1Commission proximate + ultimate + ash composition analysis on bamboo char from your specific species (B. bambos, D. strictus from Maharashtra) at a NABL-accredited lab at 3 carbonization temperatures: 500°C, 700°C, 900°CAll specs in this report are from moso bamboo or generic bamboo. Your species may differ in K content, ash composition, and FC yield. This is the foundational data gap.
2Specifically measure K₂O and Na₂O in the char ash (XRF analysis) — the alkali parameter is the single most dangerous unknownIf K₂O on ash >3%, BF injection at meaningful rates is blocked without blending dilution strategy. If <1.5%, you have a substantially cleaner pathway.
3Calculate maximum permissible blending rate: (BF alkali limit of 3 kg/tHM) ÷ (your char alkali input per kg injected) = max kg/tHM of bamboo charThis gives you the commercial ceiling for substitution rate, which drives total addressable volume and revenue per steel plant.
4Identify the right buyer contact: Raw Materials / Procurement head at RINL, JSW, SAIL, Tata Steel — not the testing tender divisionPCI coal procurement is handled by RM departments, often under 3-year contracts with international coal traders. You need to get onto approved vendor lists and into R&D co-trials, not tender systems.
5Pilot tuyere combustion test with a research partner (IIT or SAIL R&D/RDCIS Ranchi) before approaching commercial scaleSteel plants will not substitute even 5% PCI rate without combustion test data. RDCIS and TATA Steel R&D have done similar work on biomass PCI. A published or co-developed tuyere test report is the technical passport.
